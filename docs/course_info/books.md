## Readings and Resources

These resources support the course. Readings will be assigned selectively, and you are not expected to read every item cover-to-cover. Items labeled “free online” are publicly accessible; others are available through the UC Berkeley Library.

### Python and geographic data science

- **Geographic Data Science with Python** (Sergio J. Rey, Dani Arribas-Bel, Levi John Wolf, 2023). [Free online book](https://geographicdata.science/book/intro.html).
- **How to Think Like a Computer Scientist, Interactive Edition** (Runestone Interactive). [Free online interactive textbook](https://runestone.academy/ns/books/published/thinkcspy/index.html).
- **Think Python: How to Think Like a Computer Scientist** (Allen B. Downey). [Free online book](https://allendowney.github.io/ThinkPython/).
- **Dark Data: Why What You Don’t Know Matters** (David J. Hand, 2020). Online access via the UC Berkeley Library.

### Visualization

- **Visualization Analysis and Design** (Tamara Munzner, 2014). Online access via the UC Berkeley Library. [Companion materials and videos](https://www.cs.ubc.ca/~tmm/vadbook/).
- **Thematic Cartography and Geovisualization** (Terry A. Slocum, Robert B. McMaster, Fritz C. Kessler, Hugh H. Howard, 4th ed., 2022).

Additional readings may be assigned during the semester.
